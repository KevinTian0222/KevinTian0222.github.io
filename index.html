I am a high school senior at Cate School. My hometown is Chengdu, a historic city in China. I live in Carpinteria, California. Some of my hobbies include singing, soccer, and programming. Programming languages that I am familiar with include HTML, Java, and Swift. I am more than excited to learn the others in the near future, because I am fascinated with the connections between different comsci languages--how the experience in one helps one understand another more thoroughly and comprehensively.

About Me

There are other things that I enjoy--the morning breeze of Carpinteria, the splendid view on the Sunset Bench, and the stars in the clear night sky on the Mesa. I enjoy the excitement of singing and competing in sports, as well as the dedication of concentrating on a code. There are things about me that figures and words cannot possibly reflect. I can describe my experience in the school choir and jazz band, but the bland introduction cannot capture the profound moments in my life that these activities included, like the tears streaming down my face while I sing at a concert or the sense of ecstasy pulsing through my veins upon finishing a solo performance. I have found that some of the “small things” -- those parts of our life that we don’t write down in our resumes -- serve as the truest indicators of who we are.

Why DALI

Having worked with Professor Lorie Loeb over the past two years, I was fascinated by her passionate portrayal of DALI Lab’s objective to tackle obscure global issues through inspirational research and close collaboration. During my campus tour, Lorie kindly invited me to the lab, where I was intrigued by research projects like Blabl and Tarsier Goggles. I am thrilled at the prospect of joining the DALI lab, where I could collaborate with the research team on problems that people neglect and convert technical knowledge into substantial products.
